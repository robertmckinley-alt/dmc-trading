# Dumb Money Concepts (DMC) — Reference Guide

> Educational summary only. Not financial advice. Win-rate/performance claims
> referenced in the source material are the creator's own promotional
> statements and are unverified. Several rules involve subjective judgment.

## Core Premise
DMC tracks specific price *levels* rather than relying on lagging indicators.
The central idea: when price revisits a level, it either **gains** it (closes
beyond and continues) or **fails** it (rejects and reverses). This gain/fail
behavior is the primary signal for entries, exits, and trend read.

## What Is a "Level"
A level is a price point derived from a prior candle's high, low, or a
significant wick/close, usually drawn first on a higher timeframe. Levels are
marked in advance; the trader waits for price to approach before acting.

## Gain vs. Fail Mechanic
- **Gain**: candle(s) close beyond the level, confirming continuation in the
  breakout direction.
- **Fail**: price wicks into/through the level but doesn't get a clean close
  beyond it, then reverses.
Candle closes relative to the level are the confirmation tool — not a touch
or wick alone.

## Trend (DMC's Redefinition)
Trend is read as a live sequence of gains/fails at levels, not fixed
swing-structure (higher-highs/higher-lows). Repeated gains of higher levels +
failure to break lower levels = uptrend read, and vice versa.

## Multi-Timeframe Confluence / Zones
Levels are identified top-down: higher timeframe first for bias/context, then
refined on a lower timeframe for entries. When HTF and LTF levels line up, it
forms a "zone" — a higher-probability reaction area, preferred over trading
isolated levels.

## Candle Weight & Timing
Strong-bodied, decisive closes beyond a level carry more weight than small or
indecisive candles. Wait for a candle close relative to the level before
acting — don't react to a live mid-candle wick.

## Range Lock
Price chopping between a clear upper and lower level with no clean gain of
either side = "range locked." Reduce size or stand aside here. A clean gain
of either boundary is often treated as a fresh trend signal.

## Entry Styles
1. **Blind entry** — enter on first reaction at the level, no close
   confirmation. Higher risk, potentially better price.
2. **Confirmation entry** — wait for a candle close confirming gain/fail.
   Lower risk, slightly worse price.
3. **DCA entry** — scale in across a level/zone in parts rather than full
   size at once.

## Exits & Targets
Targets = the next significant level in the trade direction, trailed as
price gains successive levels (not a fixed $ or % target). Trim/exit is tied
to price approaching or failing the next level, not a time-based rule.

## Risk Management
Stop losses sit on the opposite side of the level being traded (level
failure = invalidation). Position sizing matters more than entry precision —
risk a small, defined percentage per trade; reduce size in range-locked or
choppy conditions.

## Market-Open Variant
Extra caution near market open due to volatility/false moves: favor
confirmation entries over blind entries, and wait for the first clear
gain/fail of a key level post-open before committing to a session bias.
